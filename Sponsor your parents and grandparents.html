Introduction Sponsor your parents and grandparents:

Sponsor your parents and grandparents, bringing joy and comfort to your family. As a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, you can potentially sponsor them for permanent residency in Canada.


The Family Class Sponsorship program reunites Canadian citizens and permanent residents with family members living abroad. This includes parents and grandparents who can benefit from the care and support of their loved ones in Canada.


To sponsor your parents and grandparents, you must meet government eligibility requirements:


  1. 1. You must be 18 years of age or older.
  2. 2. You must be a Canadian citizen or permanent resident.
  3. 3. You must meet the minimum income requirements to support your sponsored relative(s) and their dependents.
  4. 4. You must commit to providing financial support for your sponsored relative(s) and dependents for 3-10 years.




Once you meet the eligibility requirements, you can submit a sponsorship application on behalf of your parents or grandparents to Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). You will need to provide supporting documents, such as your birth certificate, proof of your Canadian citizenship or permanent residency, and proof of your relationship with your sponsored relative(s).


The sponsorship process can take several months or more to complete, depending on the volume of applications received by IRCC. It is important to prepare your application carefully and thoroughly, and to follow up with IRCC to ensure that your application is being processed.


If your sponsorship application is approved, your parent(s) or grandparent(s) will be issued a visa to come to Canada as permanent residents. They will have access to government-funded health care, education, and social services, just like any other Canadian resident.


Sponsor your parents and grandparents can have many benefits for your family. You will be able to provide emotional and practical support to your loved ones, and they will have the opportunity to experience Canadian culture and lifestyle. You will also benefit from their knowledge, experience, and wisdom, which can enrich your family and community.


At the same time, Sponsor your parents and grandparents can also be a significant financial and emotional commitment. You will need to provide for their basic needs, such as housing, food, clothing, and transportation, as well as their medical and other expenses. You will also need to help them adjust to Canadian society and language, and provide social and cultural support to avoid isolation and loneliness.
